What is OpenSource?

  • A bunch of software licenses
    • sourcecode is public and available for everyone
    • everyone has the opportunity of reading and modifying the sourcecode
    • license is supposed to encourage further development

History

1998

  • Increasing dominance of Internet Explorer on the browser market
  • Netscape decides to publish the sourcecode of Navigator
    • this resulted in the popular Mozilla Project

Why would one use OpenSource instead of proprietary software?

  • Most OpenSource software will cost you no money
  • Encountered bugs? Missing a feature?
    • report to the community or the developer team
  • OpenSource Software is more secure
  • OpenSource Software triggers innovation

OpenSource needs YOU!

What can YOU do?

  • The most simple thing:
    • contribute to OpenSource projects
  • Most projects are looking for testers and translators on a regular basis

What can YOU do?

  • The concept of OpenSource is portable
    • OpenAccess, OpenData
      Access to all kind of documents and information
  • Share your stuff; researches, papers, essays, etc...
    • there might be someone out there who is going to
       build something great upon it
